UNSKIPPABLE CLIMATE — FACTS DATABASE
=====================================

FORESTS
-------
1. Since 1990, the world has lost 420 million hectares of forest — an area larger than the European Union.
   Source: FAO Global Forest Resources Assessment 2020
   Link: https://www.fao.org/state-of-forests/en/

2. Tropical forests once covered 12% of Earth's land. Today they cover around 5%.
   Source: Global Forest Watch / WRI 2023
   Link: https://www.globalforestwatch.org

3. The world loses approximately 10 million hectares of forest per year — down from 16 million in 1990,
   but from a much smaller remaining base.
   Source: FAO Global Forest Resources Assessment 2020
   Link: https://www.fao.org/state-of-forests/en/


AGRICULTURE
-----------
4. Half of all habitable land on Earth is used for agriculture — and 77% of that agricultural land is
   dedicated to livestock.
   Source: Our World in Data / FAO 2023
   Link: https://ourworldindata.org/global-land-for-agriculture

5. 77% of agricultural land is used for livestock — yet animal products provide only 18% of the world's calories.
   Source: Poore & Nemecek, Science 2018
   Link: https://science.sciencemag.org/content/360/6392/987

6. Agricultural expansion drives up to 90% of global tropical deforestation.
   Source: FAO & UNEP 2020
   Link: https://www.fao.org/sustainability/news/detail/en/c/1274219/

7. A third of the world's agricultural land has been degraded by erosion in the past 40 years.
   Source: University of Sheffield / FAO 2015
   Link: https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2015/dec/02/arable-land-soil-food-security-erosion

8. Three-quarters of the world's crop species depend on pollinators — insects underpin 35% of global food
   production by volume, including most fruits, nuts, and vegetables.
   Source: FAO; Klein et al. 2007; IPBES Pollinator Assessment 2016
   Link: https://www.ipbes.net/assessment-reports/pollinators

9. Approximately 40% of invertebrate pollinator species — including bees and butterflies — are currently
   at risk of extinction.
   Source: IPBES Pollinator Assessment 2016
   Link: https://www.ipbes.net/assessment-reports/pollinators

10. The global economic value of pollination services is estimated at $235–577 billion annually — provided
    almost entirely by wild and managed insects now in steep decline.
    Source: IPBES 2016; World Economic Forum
    Link: https://www.ipbes.net/assessment-reports/pollinators


WATER
-----
11. 21 of the world's 37 largest aquifers are being depleted faster than they can naturally replenish.
    Source: Richey et al., Water Resources Research / NASA 2015
    Link: https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/2015WR017349

12. Agriculture accounts for around 70% of all freshwater withdrawals globally — livestock alone accounts
    for roughly 30% of agriculture's total water footprint, while occupying 77% of its land.
    Source: Mekonnen & Hoekstra 2012; FAO AQUASTAT
    Link: https://www.fao.org/aquastat/en/

13. The Ogallala Aquifer — which irrigates 30% of all US groundwater-fed agriculture — took millions of
    years to fill and is being drained in decades.
    Source: Scientific American / USGS 2023
    Link: https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/ogallala-aquifer/

FOOD SYSTEMS
------------
25. The world needs to produce roughly 60% more food by 2050 to feed a projected population of 9.6 billion —

    Source: Climate Impact Lab / Stanford & Rutgers, Nature 2025
    Link: https://www.nature.com/  [NEEDS DOI]

26. Every additional degree Celsius of warming reduces global food production capacity by approximately
    120 calories per person per day.
    Source: Climate Impact Lab / Stanford & Rutgers, Nature 2025
    Link: https://www.nature.com/  [NEEDS DOI]

27. Climate change is projected to reduce global staple crop yields by 8% by 2050 regardless of future
    emissions — and by 24% by 2100 under a high-emissions scenario.
    Source: Hultgren et al., Nature 2025
    Link: https://www.nature.com/  [NEEDS DOI]

    Link: https://www.woodwellclimate.org/
